I am a molecular plant biologist with a strong background in both experimental and computational plant science. My research focuses on plant–microbe interactions, molecular immune responses, and functional genomics of disease resistance—especially in the context of Fusarium wilt TR4 in banana. I work extensively with techniques including gel electrophoresis, RT-PCR, 3′ RACE, molecular cloning, real-time PCR (qPCR), transient tobacco expression, virus-induced gene silencing (VIGS), plant crossing, hydroponics cultivation, pathogen inoculation, and advanced microscopy (upright, inverted, and confocal).

On the bioinformatics side, I perform SNP calling from RNA-seq and whole-genome sequencing (WGS) data, transcriptome differential expression analysis, and WGS coverage profiling. My workflow includes scripting and data processing in R, Linux, and Python.

I also work regularly with software and tools such as: Microsoft Office, Google Workspace, Adobe Photoshop, Adobe Illustrator, Benchling, Primer3, MEGA 11, ModelTest-NG, GraphPad Prism, SnapGene, Vector NTI, EndNote, and Zen microscopy systems. My research spans several organisms, including banana, tobacco, Arabidopsis, jackfruit, rice bean, blackgram, and rice.

Outside the lab, I enjoy photography and playing badminton—activities that help me stay balanced and creative alongside my scientific work.
