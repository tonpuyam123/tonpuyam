Hello, I am Tondonba Puyam

Molecular Plant Biologist — Molecular Breeder — Fusarium wilt TR4, plant–microbe interactions, functional genomics

I am a molecular plant biologist and molecular breeder specializing in Fusarium wilt resistance, plant–microbe interactions, and functional genomics in crop plants. My research focuses on unraveling the mechanisms of resistance to Fusarium oxysporum f. sp. cubense tropical race 4 (TR4) in Cavendish banana somaclonal variants, including chromosomal deletions, susceptibility gene discovery, calcium–signaling pathways, and salicylic acid (SA)–mediated immunity.

I am currently a Ph.D. candidate in Biotechnology at National Taiwan University, conducting my research at the Agricultural Biotechnology Research Center (ABRC), Academia Sinica. Previously, I completed my M.Sc. in Genetics and Plant Breeding at the College of Post Graduate Studies in Agricultural Sciences, Central Agricultural University in Meghalaya, India, and my B.Sc. in Agriculture at Assam Agricultural University, Jorhat, India. I am actively seeking postdoctoral positions in plant molecular biology, plant pathology, functional genomics, genome editing, and host–pathogen interactions.
